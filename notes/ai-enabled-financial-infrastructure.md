# AI-Enabled Financial Infrastructure

AI is most useful in financial infrastructure when it reduces friction without reducing judgement.

The best use cases are not flashy. They are workflow-heavy, compliance-aware, and operationally valuable.

## Areas where AI can help

- summarizing complex documents
- mapping legal and regulatory issues
- preparing first drafts for expert review
- monitoring assets and portfolios
- surfacing exceptions
- improving customer support
- helping users understand financial products
- speeding up due diligence
- organizing founder-office workflows

## Design principle

AI should not replace accountability.

In regulated financial products, AI should help humans make better decisions, faster, with clearer documentation and better escalation paths.

## Relevant experience

I was exploring and promoting AI agents before the category became mainstream. My interest is practical: how AI can support real workflows in finance, product development, regulated markets, and founder/operator environments.
