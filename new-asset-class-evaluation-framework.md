# Tokenized Carbon Credit Platform

This case study is based on my work helping create and launch First Carbon / MintCarbon, a climate-fintech platform focused on tokenizing voluntary carbon credits as ERC-1155 assets on Ethereum/Polygon.

## Problem

Carbon credit holders needed a way to improve access, liquidity, transparency, and market connectivity for voluntary carbon credits.

The challenge was not simply putting a token on-chain.

The challenge was connecting a real-world environmental asset to a digital representation while preserving trust, ownership logic, registry confirmation, and asset lifecycle integrity.

## Product challenge

A credible tokenized carbon-credit workflow needed to consider:

- off-chain registry records
- on-chain asset representation
- asset holder and originator workflows
- verifier roles
- claim requests
- registry confirmation
- active credits
- retirement receipts
- burn mechanics
- marketplace connectivity
- legal and regulatory considerations

## Key design principle

The token was not the business.

The business was trusted asset lifecycle infrastructure.

A user needed to understand what they owned, what was being represented, what had been verified, what could be transferred, and what had been retired.

## Product lessons

1. Real-world assets require strong off-chain/on-chain reconciliation.
2. User experience matters because many asset owners are not crypto-native.
3. Legal and regulatory analysis has to be part of product design early.
4. Registry workflows and verifier roles need to be clear.
5. Retirement and lifecycle events are as important as initial issuance.
6. Trust is built through process clarity, not just technology.

## Relevant experience

My role involved product strategy, workflow design, market-structure thinking, legal/regulatory collaboration, and helping move a novel product toward public-company and regulator-facing execution.
