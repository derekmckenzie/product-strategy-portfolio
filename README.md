# Transaction Step Plan Framework

Complex financial products need a step plan.

A step plan turns a product concept into an executable sequence across legal, governance, financing, operating, and transaction workstreams.

## Why it matters

In early-stage financial product work, the idea is usually easier than the execution.

The hard part is sequencing:

- what needs to be formed
- who needs to approve it
- which agreements depend on other agreements
- when capital moves
- when risk transfers
- which counterparties need documents
- which tax or regulatory issues affect structure or timing
- what happens before launch
- what happens during the first transaction
- what happens after the transaction closes

## Core workstreams

### 1. Structure

- entity setup
- jurisdiction
- ownership
- capital structure
- governance

### 2. Regulatory and tax

- regulatory requirements
- cross-border issues
- tax implications
- legal characterization
- jurisdictional constraints
- counsel and tax-advisor review

### 3. Agreements

- management agreements
- administration agreements
- financing agreements
- investment guidelines
- operating policies
- counterparty agreements

### 4. Financing and capital

- share issuance or capital contribution
- loans or notes
- guarantees
- payment flows
- distribution mechanics

### 5. Operations

- onboarding
- approvals
- reporting
- servicing
- administration
- exception handling

### 6. Risk transfer

- insurance or reinsurance
- guarantees
- hedging
- downside protection
- control rights

### 7. Launch sequence

- conditions precedent
- required approvals
- transaction documents
- funds flow
- operational handoff
- post-close monitoring

## Product lesson

A step plan is not admin.

It is where strategy becomes real.
