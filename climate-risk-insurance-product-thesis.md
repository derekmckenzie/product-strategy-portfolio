# Brokerage New Assets Product Thesis

Brokerage infrastructure is moving beyond stocks and ETFs.

The next wave is not simply “more assets.” It is the ability for broker-dealers, fintechs, advisors, and developers to offer new asset classes through infrastructure that feels simple on the surface but handles complexity underneath.

## Product challenge

Adding a new asset class is not just a listing decision.

It requires decisions around:

- partner demand
- user demand
- custody
- settlement
- liquidity
- pricing
- permissions
- legal and regulatory treatment
- tax reporting
- operational support
- API design
- developer experience
- risk disclosures
- customer education

The product work is in making those pieces line up.

## My view

The best brokerage infrastructure companies will make complex financial products easier to embed, easier to understand, and easier to operate.

The winning platforms will not expose every layer of complexity to the end user. They will hide unnecessary complexity while preserving trust, compliance, transparency, and control.

## How I would evaluate a new asset class

1. Is there real customer or partner demand?
2. Is the asset understandable enough to support responsibly?
3. What custody or settlement model is required?
4. What legal, regulatory, and compliance questions need to be resolved?
5. What operational workflows will break at scale?
6. What does the API need to make easy?
7. What does the user interface need to make clear?
8. What risks need to be disclosed plainly?
9. What would make the product trustworthy?
10. What would make the product commercially worthwhile?

## Relevant experience

My background spans regulated brokerage, institutional product development, alternative assets, tokenized real-world assets, and founder/operator execution.

That mix is useful because new asset products usually fail when product, legal, market structure, operations, and customer experience are treated separately.
