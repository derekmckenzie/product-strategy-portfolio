# Regulated Product Launch Checklist

Complex financial products should not be treated as normal software launches.

The product, legal, regulatory, operational, commercial, and customer experience questions need to be worked through together.

## 1. Product

- What problem are we solving?
- Who is the user?
- What decision or workflow are we improving?
- What needs to be simple?
- What complexity can be hidden?
- What complexity must be disclosed?

## 2. Market structure

- What asset, transaction, or financial exposure is being created?
- Who owns what?
- Who holds custody or control?
- How does settlement work?
- Where does liquidity come from?
- What happens when something fails?

## 3. Legal and regulatory

- What legal characterizations are possible?
- What is the most conservative interpretation?
- What needs external counsel review?
- What disclosures are required?
- What regulator expectations need to be considered?
- What cannot be said in marketing?

## 4. Operations

- Who approves what?
- What manual processes exist at launch?
- What needs to be automated later?
- What breaks if volume increases?
- What controls need to exist?
- What support issues will customers have?

## 5. User experience

- What does the user need to know?
- What does the user not need to know?
- Where could the user misunderstand the product?
- What language makes the risk clear?
- What creates trust?

## 6. Commercial model

- Who pays?
- Why do they pay?
- What is the margin structure?
- What incentives could create bad behaviour?
- What does success look like after 6, 12, and 24 months?

## 7. Launch readiness

- Is the product legally reviewed?
- Are key workflows documented?
- Are risk disclosures clear?
- Are operational owners assigned?
- Are escalation paths defined?
- Are customer-facing materials accurate?
- Are we launching a product or just a feature?
