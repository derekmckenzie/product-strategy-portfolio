# Regulated Digital Assets Strategy

Digital asset products need to be designed as financial products, not just technology products.

The best opportunities are where the product solves a real market problem and the technology improves ownership, access, transparency, settlement, or workflow.

## Product challenge

Regulated digital asset products often fail because teams separate the product, legal, compliance, market structure, custody, and user experience questions.

Those questions need to be solved together.

## Evaluation framework

When evaluating a regulated digital asset product, I would ask:

1. What real-world problem is being solved?
2. What asset, claim, exposure, or workflow is being represented?
3. Who owns what?
4. Where is custody or control held?
5. What off-chain records need to be reconciled?
6. What legal characterization is most likely?
7. What is the conservative regulatory view?
8. What user misunderstanding is most dangerous?
9. What operational workflow needs to be trusted?
10. What would make the product useful without relying on hype?

## Product principles

- Start with the user problem, not the token.
- Design the legal/regulatory path early.
- Make ownership and rights understandable.
- Do not hide key risks.
- Use blockchain only where it improves the system.
- Treat trust, auditability, and operations as product features.

## Relevant experience

My experience includes tokenized real-world asset workflows, securities/derivatives analysis with counsel, regulated brokerage, institutional product development, and founder/operator execution.
