# Self-Custodied Crypto Bundle Product Thesis

This is a sanitized product strategy note based on early work around a self-custodied crypto bundle concept.

## Problem

Broad crypto exposure is still harder than it should be.

Many users understand the idea of diversified exposure from ETFs and index funds, but crypto investing often pushes them toward a poor set of choices:

- concentrate in one or two major tokens
- use a centralized platform and give up control
- manually buy many assets across fragmented venues
- use DeFi tools that feel built for developers
- accept a poor user experience to achieve diversification

The opportunity is to make diversified crypto exposure feel closer to index investing while preserving user control.

## Product concept

A user connects their wallet, selects a pre-built bundle or custom allocation, reviews the strategy, and confirms a transaction.

The infrastructure then executes the bundle through smart contracts so the user receives the underlying assets in their own wallet.

The key product idea is not just “buy multiple tokens.”

The key idea is to give the user a simple execution layer for a diversified strategy while keeping custody and ownership with the user.

## Product challenge

The product needs to balance several tensions:

- simple experience vs. complex execution
- user control vs. guardrails
- self-custody vs. support expectations
- smart-contract automation vs. transparency
- pre-built bundles vs. user customization
- market-cap weighting vs. equal weighting
- gas cost vs. execution quality
- education vs. overwhelming detail
- compliance posture vs. product simplicity

## User flow

1. Connect wallet
2. Select a bundle
3. Adjust or opt out of specific assets
4. Review allocation and execution assumptions
5. Confirm transaction
6. Smart contract executes purchases
7. Assets settle into the user’s wallet
8. User can monitor, rebalance, or unwind later

## Key design principle

The user should feel in control without being forced to understand every technical step.

The product should make the execution easy, but not hide what matters: custody, allocation, risks, fees, smart-contract behaviour, and asset ownership.

## Product questions to solve

- What assets are eligible for inclusion?
- Who determines the default bundles?
- Are bundles market-cap weighted, equal weighted, or user-defined?
- How is rebalancing handled?
- How are fees disclosed?
- How is slippage handled?
- What happens if one leg of the transaction fails?
- How does the product avoid giving investment advice?
- What are the relevant securities, derivatives, and platform-regulation considerations?
- What does the user own after execution?
- What support issues arise if the user controls the wallet?

## Why this matters

Self-custodied financial products need better interfaces.

If crypto infrastructure is going to be useful beyond early adopters, products need to combine familiar financial concepts with transparent execution and user-controlled ownership.
