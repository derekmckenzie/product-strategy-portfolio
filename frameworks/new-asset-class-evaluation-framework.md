# New Asset Class Evaluation Framework

Adding a new asset class is a full product, legal, operational, commercial, and infrastructure decision.

This framework is meant to organize the work before a platform commits to launch.

## 1. Demand

- Who wants this asset class?
- Is the demand from users, partners, developers, advisors, or institutions?
- Is demand durable or trend-driven?
- What problem does access solve?

## 2. Product fit

- Does the asset class fit the platform’s brand and trust model?
- Can it be explained clearly?
- Does it create confusion with existing products?
- Does it improve the platform or distract from it?

## 3. Market structure

- Where does liquidity come from?
- How is pricing determined?
- How does order routing or execution work?
- What are the trading hours?
- What happens in stressed markets?

## 4. Custody and settlement

- Who holds the asset?
- How does settlement occur?
- What are the failure modes?
- Are there reconciliation requirements?
- How are corporate actions, lifecycle events, or distributions handled?

## 5. Legal and regulatory

- What is the likely legal characterization?
- What licences, registrations, or exemptions matter?
- What disclosures are needed?
- What jurisdictions create the most risk?
- What does counsel need to confirm?

## 6. Operations

- What workflows are manual at launch?
- What teams need to support it?
- What breaks at scale?
- What monitoring is required?
- What happens when customers ask basic questions?

## 7. Developer and API experience

- What needs to be easy for developers?
- What needs to be visible in the API?
- What objects, events, or states need to exist?
- What errors need to be clear?
- What documentation will prevent misuse?

## 8. Commercial model

- How does the platform make money?
- What fees are acceptable?
- What partner economics matter?
- What incentives could create bad behaviour?
- What is the expected contribution to growth or retention?

## 9. Trust

- What makes the product credible?
- What could make the platform look careless?
- What are users likely to misunderstand?
- What should be said plainly?

## 10. Launch decision

Launch only when the product can be explained simply, operated reliably, supported responsibly, and defended under legal/regulatory review.
